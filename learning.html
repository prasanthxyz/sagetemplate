SAT:

What is the SAT?

Do you have that exceptional academic skill? Are you ready to cross borders for an extraordinary educational opportunity? Then the SAT is for you.

The Scholastic Aptitude Test lets you showcase your academic intelligence, opening admission opportunities at thousands of universities in the United States and American universities globally.

What does the SAT test?

The SAT assesses skills in Reading Comprehension, Grammar, Analytical Writing, and Math.

The SAT tests college readiness and proficiency along with key areas of Verbal and Math skills, but it is also a test of logic, and answers are designed to confused students. In order to do well on the SAT, it is essential to learn key strategies and techniques to essentially outsmart the test.

The exam is comprised of 3 tests -- Evidence-based reading and writing sections, Math sections, and an optional essay. The overall exam is 4 hours and 10 minutes and includes 3 short breaks.

Students can take the SAT a maximum of 5 times, with most students taking the exam 1-3 times. Score are valid for 5 years.

THE SAT OR ACT DILEMMA

A question we often hear from students is, which test should I take? The answer is different for each student.

In 2016, the SAT was modified to better reflect what students are learning in school. Analogy questions that required memorizing hundreds of vocabulary words were removed, and the math tests increased in scope to include statistics and probability.

However, the new SAT verbal sections are reading intensive and do require thorough studying of vocabulary and content in order to perform well. The ACT reading section is less content intensive, but students have less time per question.

The quantitative sections on the SAT and ACT require an understating of a similar range of topics. Some crucial differences are that the ACT contains science and the questions tend to be more straightforward. The ACT tests students on their fundamental knowledge rather than their ability to perform well on exams.

The Writing and Language, or English ACT test renders more focus on grammar and logic and does not require students to study vocabulary as intensely as the SAT does.

Finally, the SAT essay evaluates students' rhetorical analysis skills, while the ACT essay tests critical thinking.

Which exam you decide is crucial, and Sage is here to guide you through the process. Your success is our success. Sage is here to help you to help you accomplish your goals and make sure that the profile you send to colleges highlights your most unique, and impressive qualities.

WHAT is A GOOD SAT SCORE?

There isn't a specific score that classifies as "GOOD." The score you need depends on universities you are applying to, their requirements, and your overall profile.

Universities consider your profile including grades, extracurricular activities, and any distinctive talents in order to determine acceptance. The SAT is not the only factor, but it is an important one. A high SAT score also makes students eligible for competitive scholarships.

The average score for the new SAT is 1000. That implies that, if you score above 1000 you are scoring higher than 50 percent of test takers. However, this also means that half of all test takers are scoring higher than you.
